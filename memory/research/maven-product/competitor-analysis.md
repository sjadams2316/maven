# Maven Product: Competitor Analysis

*Research compiled: February 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

The personal finance and wealth management space is crowded but fragmented. No single player combines **AI-native intelligence**, **deep tax optimization**, **transparent reasoning**, and **truly personalized advice**. This creates Maven's opportunity.

| Competitor | Model | Fee | Strength | Gap |
|------------|-------|-----|----------|-----|
| Wealthfront | AUM | 0.25% | Tax-loss harvesting automation | No personalization, no human touch |
| Betterment | AUM | 0.25-0.65% | Goals-based + human advisor tier | Commoditized, generic advice |
| Empower | AUM + Free | 0.89% | Best free aggregation tools | High fees, pushy upselling |
| Copilot Money | Subscription | $95/yr | Beautiful UX | No investing, just tracking |
| Monarch | Subscription | $99/yr | Robust budgeting | No investment guidance |
| Mezzi | Subscription | ~$100/yr | AI-native tax focus | Early stage, limited features |

## 1. Wealthfront

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2008 (one of the first robo-advisors)
- **AUM:** ~$60B+ (as of late 2025)
- **Users:** 800,000+ clients
- **Revenue (2023):** ~$184M (Sacra estimate)
- **Status:** Filed for IPO in September 2025; targeting $1.8B-$2.1B valuation

### Business Model
- **Advisory Fee:** 0.25% annually on all invested assets
- **Cash Accounts:** ~0.40% effective yield capture (higher margin than advisory)
- **No minimums:** Free for first $10K managed historically
- **Revenue drivers:** Now primarily cash accounts (high-yield savings), with robo-advisory as growth driver

### Key Features
1. **Tax-Loss Harvesting (TLH)**
   - Daily automated monitoring
   - Claims ~1.8% boost to after-tax returns
   - Generated $49.83M in estimated client tax benefit in 2024
   - Works on ETF-level and individual stock level (with direct indexing)

2. **Direct Indexing (US Direct / S&P 500 Direct)**
   - Hold individual stocks instead of ETFs
   - More tax-loss harvesting opportunities
   - Minimum $100K for US Direct, lower for S&P 500 Direct
   - Smart Beta strategies available

3. **Cash Management**
   - High-yield cash account (competitive APY)
   - FDIC insurance through partner banks
   - Drives majority of new customer acquisition

4. **Planning Tools**
   - Path (retirement planning)
   - College savings (529)
   - Home planning
   - Self Driving Money‚Ñ¢ (automated allocation)

5. **Recent Moves (2024-2025)**
   - S&P 500 Direct launched
   - Stock lending program (extra income)
   - Home lending (mortgages) in beta
   - IPO filing

### What They Do WELL ‚úÖ
- **Automation excellence:** Truly hands-off investing
- **Tax-loss harvesting:** Industry-leading, daily monitoring, documented results
- **Clean UX:** Well-designed app and web experience
- **Transparent fees:** Simple 0.25%, no hidden costs
- **Cash management:** Competitive rates, good integration
- **Tax efficiency:** Combines TLH, asset location, dividend reinvestment

### Where They Fall SHORT ‚ùå
- **No personalization:** Cookie-cutter portfolios based on simple questionnaire
- **No human advisors:** Zero access to CFPs or financial planners
- **Black box decisions:** Users don't understand *why* trades happen
- **Complex situations ignored:** RSUs, options, concentrated stock positions, estate planning
- **Limited customization:** Can't easily tilt or customize strategies
- **Aggregation weak:** Poor external account visibility compared to Empower

### User Complaints (Reddit/Reviews)
- "Hard to see realized vs unrealized gains"
- "Financial aggregation apps can't pull individual positions properly"
- "No way to understand the reasoning behind trades"
- "Doesn't handle stock compensation well"
- "TLH stops working effectively in long bull markets"

## 2. Betterment

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** 2010
- **AUM:** ~$65B+ (as of late 2025)
- **Users:** 1M+ customers
- **Revenue (2023):** ~$153M (Sacra estimate)
- **Status:** Private, last valued at $1.15B

### Business Model
- **Digital Plan:** 0.25% annually (as of Jan 2026: $5/month flat for accounts <$24K)
- **Premium Plan:** 0.65% annually ($100K minimum) - includes CFP access
- **B2B (Betterment at Work):** 401(k) for employers
- **Betterment Advisor Solutions (B4A):** RIA custody platform

### Key Features
1. **Goals-Based Investing**
   - Multiple goals with separate allocations
   - Retirement, home purchase, emergency fund, custom
   - Visual progress tracking

2. **Tax Coordination**
   - Tax-loss harvesting (enabled by default)
   - Tax-coordinated portfolio (asset location across accounts)
   - Tax Impact Preview (shows pre-trade tax implications)

3. **Human Advisor Access (Premium)**
   - Unlimited CFP consultations at 0.65% tier
   - Financial planning packages
   - Discontinued √† la carte sessions in 2024

4. **401(k) Business (Betterment at Work)**
   - Student loan matching
   - Full recordkeeping
   - Partnership with First Citizens Wealth (2025)

5. **B4A/Betterment Advisor Solutions**
   - RIA custody and portfolio management
   - Mutual fund support added 2024
   - Direct indexing via Rowboat acquisition (2025)
   - Integrations: eMoney, Kwanti, Panoramix

6. **Recent Moves (2024-2025)**
   - Premium fee increase (0.40% ‚Üí 0.65%)
   - Acquired Ellevest automated investing (~$1.1B AUM)
   - Acquired Rowboat Advisors (direct indexing)
   - Launched self-directed investing (commission-free)
   - Solo 401(k) product

### What They Do WELL ‚úÖ
- **Goals-based framework:** Intuitive mental model for users
- **Human advisor option:** Premium tier fills the "I want to talk to someone" need
- **Tax coordination:** Cross-account optimization
- **Employer channel:** Strong 401(k) business creates captive customers
- **B4A platform:** Growing RIA custody business
- **Tax Impact Preview:** Shows what a trade will cost before execution

### Where They Fall SHORT ‚ùå
- **Generic advice:** Same portfolios for everyone with similar risk tolerance
- **Premium is expensive:** 0.65% for CFP access is steep
- **Limited customization:** Less flexible than Wealthfront
- **Commoditized:** Little differentiation from Schwab/Vanguard robo
- **No crypto:** Can invest in trusts but not actual tokens
- **TLH effectiveness questioned:** Some users report no harvesting for 2+ years in bull markets

### User Complaints (Reddit/Reviews)
- "No TLH for over 2 years in my account"
- "Premium fee increase was unexpected"
- "Better options for DIY investors at Fidelity/Schwab"
- "Feels like they're pivoting away from retail"

## 3. Empower (formerly Personal Capital)

### Company Overview
- **Original:** Personal Capital founded 2009
- **Acquired:** By Empower Retirement in 2020 for $1B
- **Parent:** Great-West Lifeco (Canadian insurance giant)
- **Strategy:** Free tools as funnel to wealth management upsell

### Business Model
- **Free Tier:** Account aggregation, net worth tracking, dashboards, retirement planner
- **Wealth Management:**
  - 0.89% for accounts $100K-$1M
  - Tiered lower fees above $1M
  - Requires minimum $100K (investment services) or $250K (full wealth management)
  - Includes dedicated human advisors

### Key Features
1. **Account Aggregation** (Free)
   - Connect banks, brokerages, 401(k), mortgage, loans, credit cards
   - Real-time net worth tracking
   - Best-in-class dashboard
   - Crypto tracking (manual entry)

2. **Retirement Planner** (Free)
   - Monte Carlo simulations
   - Multiple scenario comparison
   - Social Security optimization
   - Income/spending projections

3. **Fee Analyzer** (Free)
   - Calculates weighted expense ratios
   - Shows 10+ year fee impact
   - Excellent for portfolio analysis

4. **Investment Checkup** (Free)
   - Asset allocation analysis
   - Compares to recommended allocation
   - Sector/geographic exposure

5. **Wealth Management** (Paid)
   - Dedicated human advisors (2 for accounts $250K+)
   - Tax-loss harvesting
   - Individual stocks and ETFs
   - College savings, estate planning

### What They Do WELL ‚úÖ
- **Best free aggregation:** Unmatched for seeing everything in one place
- **Beautiful dashboards:** Clean, informative, well-designed
- **Retirement planning tools:** Robust and free
- **Fee analyzer:** Eye-opening for most users
- **Human advisors included:** Real people at 0.89% tier

### Where They Fall SHORT ‚ùå
- **High fees:** 0.89% is 3.5x Wealthfront/Betterment
- **Pushy sales:** Constant calls to "schedule a consultation"
- **Aggressive upselling:** Free users get pestered
- **$100K minimum:** Excludes mass market
- **Slow innovation:** Acquisition seems to have slowed product development
- **Generic advice:** Human advisors often give boilerplate recommendations

### User Complaints (Reddit/Reviews)
- "The 'free review' was just a sales pitch"
- "They call way too much after signing up"
- "Advice was generic, could have been put together in 30 minutes"
- "Not pushy but definitely trying to sell"
- "Free tools are great, wealth management is overpriced"

## 4. Copilot Money

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** ~2019
- **Users:** Unknown (growing quickly post-Mint shutdown)
- **Platform:** iOS, Mac, Web (Apple ecosystem focus)
- **Recognition:** Apple Design Award Finalist, Editor's Choice

### Business Model
- **Subscription:** $95/year (or $13/month)
- **No free tier:** Trial available, then paid
- **No AUM fees:** Pure subscription model
- **No investing:** Tracking/budgeting only

### Key Features
1. **Transaction Tracking**
   - AI-powered auto-categorization
   - Learning from user corrections
   - Clean transaction views

2. **Budgeting**
   - Spending targets by category
   - Rollover budgets
   - Monthly trends

3. **Net Worth Tracking**
   - Connect all accounts
   - Investment tracking (view-only)
   - Real estate via Zillow integration

4. **Subscription Detection**
   - Identifies recurring charges
   - Upcoming bill reminders

5. **Design Quality**
   - Native Apple experience
   - Beautiful animations
   - Praised by MKBHD, design community

### What They Do WELL ‚úÖ
- **Outstanding UX:** Best-designed finance app, period
- **Apple-native:** Feels like it belongs in the ecosystem
- **AI categorization:** Learns quickly, mostly accurate
- **Simple philosophy:** Does tracking/budgeting well, nothing else
- **Privacy-focused:** No ads, no data selling

### Where They Fall SHORT ‚ùå
- **No investing features:** View-only, no advice or management
- **Expensive for just budgeting:** $95/year for what Mint did free
- **Limited feature depth:** Compared to Monarch or YNAB
- **Couples/household support weak:** Clunky for joint finances
- **No transaction import:** Can't bring in historical data
- **Rollover feature criticized:** Implementation has issues
- **No Android:** Apple ecosystem only

### User Complaints (Reddit/Reviews)
- "$95/year is a lot for a basic budgeting app"
- "Can't import transactions from other apps"
- "Rollover feature is useless as implemented"
- "Tons of time recategorizing transactions"
- "Great design but missing key features"
- "Only on Apple - no Android support"

### Target Market
- Design-conscious millennials
- Apple ecosystem users
- Mint refugees seeking quality UX
- People who want simple tracking, not active management

## 5. Other Competitors

### Monarch Money
- **Price:** $99/year or $14.99/month
- **Focus:** Comprehensive budgeting and financial tracking
- **Strengths:**
  - Multiple budgeting styles (envelope, traditional)
  - Custom categories with emoji
  - Strong household/couples support
  - Investment tracking
  - Goal tracking
  - Cross-platform (web, iOS, Android)
- **Weaknesses:**
  - No investing management
  - Pricey vs free alternatives
  - Learning curve
- **Position:** The "serious" Mint replacement for power users

### Kubera
- **Price:** $249/year (Essentials) or $2,499/year (Black)
- **Target:** High-net-worth individuals (HNIs)
- **Focus:** Comprehensive net worth tracking across complex portfolios
- **Strengths:**
  - Handles complex assets (alts, LP positions, private equity)
  - Multi-currency support
  - Multiple entity structures (trusts, LLCs)
  - AI import from documents/screenshots
  - Dead man's switch (beneficiary transfer)
  - Club Benchmarks (compare to similar portfolios)
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Very expensive
  - No investment advice
  - Tracking only, no management
- **Position:** The spreadsheet replacement for wealthy self-managers

### Boldin (formerly New Retirement)
- **Price:** ~$12/month (PlannerPlus) or $120/year
- **Focus:** DIY retirement planning
- **Strengths:**
  - Extremely detailed planning tools
  - Social Security optimization
  - Roth conversion modeling
  - IRMAA impact analysis
  - Multiple scenario comparison
  - Monte Carlo simulations
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Steep learning curve
  - Planning only, no implementation
  - No investment management
- **Position:** For DIY planners who want CFP-level tools

### Mezzi
- **Price:** Subscription-based (exact pricing unclear, freemium available)
- **Focus:** AI-native wealth advice with tax optimization focus
- **Strengths:**
  - SEC-registered investment advisor
  - AI-powered tax optimization recommendations
  - Cross-account portfolio analysis
  - Retirement planning
  - Roth vs Traditional optimization
  - Wash sale tracking
  - Claims 1-2% annual return boost from tax optimization
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Early stage, feature-limited
  - AI recommendations but no execution
  - Smaller user base, less proven
- **Position:** The AI-native alternative to paying an advisor

### Other Mentions
- **YNAB:** $99/year, envelope budgeting, cult following, no investing
- **Simplifi (Quicken):** $48/year, budgeting, basic investment tracking
- **Personal Finance Club:** Educational content, not a product
- **Tiller:** $79/year, spreadsheet-based, for power users

## 6. Maven's Differentiation & Opportunity

### What None of Them Do Well

1. **True Personalization**
   - Robo-advisors give everyone the same portfolios
   - They don't understand YOUR specific tax situation, stock comp, concentrated positions
   - "Your risk tolerance is 7/10" ‚Üí here's your generic portfolio

2. **Transparent Reasoning**
   - Wealthfront/Betterment: "We did some tax-loss harvesting" (no explanation)
   - Users never understand WHY a decision was made
   - No education, no learning, no confidence building

3. **Complex Situation Handling**
   - RSUs, ISOs, NSOs ‚Üí robo-advisors ignore these
   - Concentrated stock positions ‚Üí generic "diversify" advice
   - Cross-account optimization ‚Üí partial at best
   - Life events (divorce, inheritance, career change) ‚Üí not considered

4. **Proactive Guidance**
   - Current tools are reactive: you ask, they answer
   - Nobody says "Hey, you should do X before year-end"
   - No monitoring of your situation for opportunities

5. **Integration of Planning + Execution**
   - Planners (Boldin) tell you what to do, you figure out how
   - Robo-advisors execute, but don't explain or plan deeply
   - Gap between "here's your plan" and "here's it happening"

### AI-Native vs AI-Assisted

| AI-Assisted | AI-Native |
|-------------|-----------|
| AI added to existing workflow | AI IS the core experience |
| Chatbot on top of dashboard | Every interaction powered by intelligence |
| "Ask our AI" feature | AI anticipates, suggests, explains |
| Limited to FAQ/support | Reasoning permeates everything |
| Wealthfront, Betterment, Copilot | **Maven opportunity** |

Mezzi is trying to be AI-native but is early and focused primarily on tax optimization. The full potential of AI-native wealth management is untapped.

### Tax Optimization Depth

Current state of tax optimization:
- **Wealthfront/Betterment:** Tax-loss harvesting (ETF swaps), basic asset location
- **Mezzi:** More sophisticated, cross-account view, but advisory only
- **Empower:** Basic TLH for paid clients
- **Others:** Nothing

What's missing:
- **Holistic tax planning:** Seeing W-2, cap gains, dividends, deductions together
- **Life event optimization:** RSU vesting timing, option exercise strategies
- **Year-end planning:** "You should realize this loss to offset that gain"
- **State tax considerations:** Moving, multi-state income
- **Future tax rate predictions:** Roth conversion timing

### Transparency of Reasoning

What users get today:
- "We harvested $X in losses this year" (Wealthfront)
- "Your portfolio is allocated 60/40" (Betterment)
- Graphs without explanation

What they want:
- "We sold VTI and bought ITOT because VTI was down 3% and this will save you approximately $500 in taxes this year, without changing your market exposure"
- "You're 45 and your kids are 12 and 15, so we've started shifting toward bonds because..."
- "Given your salary and expected bonus, we think Roth contributions make more sense than Traditional this year because..."

### The Maven Opportunity

**Position:** The AI-native wealth platform that combines:
1. Deep, personalized understanding of your situation
2. Transparent reasoning you can learn from
3. Proactive guidance, not just reactive tools
4. Tax optimization beyond basic TLH
5. Handles complexity that robo-advisors can't

**Value Proposition:**
- "What a great financial advisor does, at scale"
- "Understand every decision made with your money"
- "Optimized for YOUR situation, not a questionnaire bucket"

**Key Differentiators:**
1. AI that EXPLAINS its reasoning
2. Handles RSUs, ISOs, concentrated positions, complex income
3. Proactive alerts and suggestions
4. Cross-account optimization that actually works
5. Learning experience: users get smarter over time

**Competitive Moats:**
- Depth of personalization (hard to replicate)
- Reasoning transparency (requires different architecture)
- User trust through understanding (compounds over time)
- Handling complexity (where robo-advisors explicitly fail)
