# RIA Technology Landscape Research
*Last Updated: February 2026*

## Executive Summary

The RIA technology landscape is mature but fragmented. Advisors use an average of 2.0 platforms (down from 2.2 in 2024), showing a trend toward consolidation. **71% of advisors cite lack of integration as their top frustration**. The market is ripe for disruption from a truly unified platform that addresses the gaps left by incumbent players.

## 5. Client Portals

### What Clients Actually Use & Expect

**Current State:**
- Most portals are functional but uninspiring
- Often tied to platform (Orion, Tamarac, Black Diamond, Advyzon all have native portals)
- Account aggregation becoming table stakes
- Mobile experience increasingly important

**Client Expectations (especially younger):**
- Real-time account visibility
- Goal tracking & progress visualization
- Document vault
- Secure messaging
- Mobile-first design
- Seamless aggregation of held-away accounts
- Modern, consumer-grade UX (think Robinhood, not 1990s)

**Gap:** 91% of wealthy clients want estate planning advice, only 22% receive it. Portals don't typically surface planning insights proactively.

## 6. Major Pain Points RIAs Face

### 1. **Integration Fragmentation (71% cite as top frustration)**
- Data doesn't flow between systems
- Manual re-entry across platforms
- Reconciliation nightmares
- "Frankenstack" problem

### 2. **Data Aggregation & Quality**
- #1 factor in tech selection (Cerulli 2024)
- Held-away accounts difficult to aggregate
- Data latency issues
- Alternative investments particularly challenging

### 3. **Onboarding & Implementation**
- Complex setups (especially Salesforce, Black Diamond)
- Data migration painful
- Training burden

### 4. **Cost & Value**
- Enterprise tools expensive for small/mid RIAs
- Unclear ROI
- Per-user pricing adds up quickly

### 5. **Outdated UX**
- Legacy interfaces
- Not mobile-optimized
- Client portals feel like afterthoughts

### 6. **Compliance & Cybersecurity**
- Only 58% use cybersecurity software (alarming)
- Compliance features often bolted on, not native

### 7. **Scalability**
- Tools that work for 50 clients break at 500
- Enterprise features priced out of reach

## 7. AI Integration in Wealth Management

### Current State (2024-2025)
- **54%** believe AI will have greatest impact on industry growth
- **Only 23%** have implemented AI at their own firm
- **~60%** use at least one AI-enabled feature

### Primary AI Use Cases

**1. Note-Taking & Meeting Summaries (#1 application)**
- Tools: Wealthbox AI notetaker, FinMate, Jump
- PreciseFP saved 198,012 hours in 2025 via automation

**2. Client Communications**
- Drafting emails, newsletters
- ChatGPT, Claude usage high
- Compliance review of AI-generated content still manual

**3. Data Entry & Document Processing**
- Holistiplan OCR for tax returns
- Estate document scanning
- Account opening automation

**4. Research & Analysis**
- Investment research summarization
- Market commentary generation
- AI tools: ChatGPT (35.71% market share), Copilot (12.12%), Gemini (6.86%)
- Highest rated: Perplexity (8.26/10), Anthropic Claude (8.10/10)

### Emerging AI Applications
- Portfolio optimization
- Tax-loss harvesting recommendations
- Risk profiling
- Next-best-action for clients
- Sentiment analysis on client communications

### AI Integration Gap
Most AI is "bolted on" via standalone tools. **No major platform has deeply integrated AI into the core workflow yet.**

## 9. Integration Requirements

### Custodian Connections (Critical)

**"Big Three" Custodians:**
1. **Charles Schwab** (includes former TD Ameritrade)
   - Schwab Advisor Center
   - Direct feeds available
   - iRebal integration
   
2. **Fidelity Institutional**
   - ~3,400 advisory firms
   - $3.9T AUA
   - Strong API program
   
3. **BNY Pershing**
   - API-based data strategy
   - Wealth Solutions (RIAs) + Institutional Solutions
   - Complex but comprehensive

**Rising Custodians:**
- **Altruist** - jumped to 6.25% market share (from 2.85%)
- **TradePMR** - strong integration focus
- **Apex** - API-first modern platform

### Data Feed Types
1. **Position data** - Holdings, values, cost basis
2. **Transaction data** - Buys, sells, dividends, fees
3. **Account data** - Account info, registration
4. **Performance data** - TWR, IRR calculations
5. **Document data** - Statements, confirms

### Key Integration Partners
- **Account Aggregation:** Yodlee (Envestnet), Plaid, MX, Finicity
- **Planning:** eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, RightCapital
- **CRM:** Redtail, Wealthbox, Salesforce
- **Risk:** Nitrogen/Riskalyze, HiddenLevers
- **E-Signatures:** DocuSign, Adobe Sign
- **Compliance:** RIA in a Box, SmartRIA

### API Standards & Considerations
- Most use REST APIs
- Data normalization is the hard problem
- Real-time vs. batch (most are T+1)
- OAuth 2.0 for authentication
- Consider Open Financial Exchange (OFX) for some legacy systems

## 10. Key Takeaways for Maven

1. **Integration is the #1 pain point** - build for it from day one
2. **AI is the opportunity** - no one has done it right yet
3. **Client experience is stuck in 2010** - modern UX is a differentiator
4. **Planning-to-portfolio gap** - connecting planning to execution is valuable
5. **Smaller RIAs underserved** - affordable, simple, all-in-one wins
6. **Estate planning gap is massive** - 91% want it, 22% get it
7. **Advyzon is the model** - all-in-one, modern, high satisfaction
8. **Custodian-agnostic is essential** - don't lock into one relationship
9. **Compliance/security as feature** - only 58% use cybersecurity tools
10. **Mobile-first for next-gen** - both advisors and clients expect it
