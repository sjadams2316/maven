# Multi-Family Office Services: Complete Breakdown for $50M+ Families

**Research Date:** February 7, 2026  
**Sources:** Campden Wealth, RBC/Campden Family Office Reports 2024-2025, J.P. Morgan Private Bank, UBS Global Family Office Report, industry practitioners

---

## Executive Summary

Multi-family offices (MFOs) represent the most comprehensive wealth management solution available for ultra-high-net-worth families, typically those with $25M-$100M+ in investable assets. Unlike traditional wealth management, MFOs provide an integrated, holistic approach spanning investments, tax, estate planning, family governance, lifestyle services, and generational education—all coordinated through a single relationship.

This report breaks down exactly what MFOs provide, what it costs, how the $50M+ family experience differs from retail wealth management, and identifies opportunities to democratize elements of these services for broader accessibility.

## 2. What MFOs Provide Beyond Investments

### The Integration Advantage

The fundamental differentiator of MFOs versus traditional wealth management is **integration**. Traditional advisors operate in silos—your investment manager doesn't talk to your tax attorney, who doesn't coordinate with your estate planner. MFOs serve as the "quarterback," ensuring:

- Tax implications are considered before investment decisions
- Estate plans align with current asset structure
- Insurance coverage matches actual risk exposure
- Family governance supports smooth succession
- Philanthropy integrates with tax and legacy goals

### Specific Non-Investment Services

**Administrative Services**
According to Wikipedia's comprehensive family office definition, MFOs commonly provide:
- Bill pay services (managing household expenses, property taxes, insurance premiums)
- Personal financial statement preparation
- Document management and recordkeeping
- Cash management across accounts and entities
- Foundation management and grant administration

**Coordination Services**
MFOs maintain relationships with and coordinate:
- External investment managers ("best of breed" selection)
- Tax attorneys and CPAs
- Estate planning attorneys
- Insurance brokers
- Real estate advisors
- Trust companies
- Philanthropic advisors

**Proactive Planning**
Unlike reactive advisory, MFOs provide:
- Continuous monitoring for tax law changes affecting the family
- Proactive restructuring recommendations
- Regular estate plan reviews (not just at death or major life events)
- Multi-generational planning across 3+ generations
- Family education before wealth transfers occur

## 3. Tax Planning Depth at This Level

### UHNW Tax Strategies Employed by Family Offices

Family offices utilize sophisticated strategies unavailable or impractical for smaller portfolios:

**Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs)**
- Transfer appreciation to heirs with minimal/zero gift tax
- "Zeroed-out GRATs" transfer value at nearly zero taxable gift value
- Rolling 2-year GRATs minimize mortality risk
- Ideal for pre-IPO stock, concentrated positions, expected appreciation
- *Example: Mark Zuckerberg used GRATs to transfer billions in pre-IPO Facebook stock*

**Intentionally Defective Grantor Trusts (IDGTs)**
- Grantor pays income tax on trust income (tax-free growth for beneficiaries)
- Assets removed from taxable estate
- Ability to swap assets in/out for flexibility
- Minority interest discounts reduce reportable gift value

**Dynasty Trusts**
- Multi-generational wealth transfer (can last hundreds of years in favorable jurisdictions)
- Leverage GST tax exemption ($13.99M per individual in 2025)
- Assets grow outside taxable estates of all descendants
- Asset protection from creditors, divorce, poor decisions
- Requires jurisdiction selection (South Dakota, Nevada, Delaware)

**Family Limited Partnerships (FLPs)**
- Consolidate family assets for management efficiency
- Valuation discounts for minority interests (15-35% discounts)
- Creditor protection
- Structured succession and control mechanisms

**Charitable Strategies**
- **Charitable Remainder Trusts (CRTs):** Income stream + charitable deduction + capital gains deferral
- **Donor-Advised Funds:** Immediate deduction, flexible future giving
- **Private Foundations:** Maximum control, ongoing deductions
- **Charitable Lead Trusts:** Reduce estate/gift taxes on wealth transfer

**Private Placement Life Insurance (PPLI)**
- Insurance "wrapper" around tax-inefficient investments
- Tax-deferred growth on hedge funds, private credit
- Tax-free access via policy loans
- Tax-free death benefit
- Trust ownership removes from estate

**Opportunity Zone Investments**
- Defer and potentially eliminate capital gains
- 10-year hold eliminates tax on new appreciation
- Requires reinvestment of capital gains into Qualified Opportunity Fund

### Tax Planning Coordination

At the MFO level, tax planning is:
- **Year-round** (not just April)
- **Multi-entity** (coordinating across trusts, partnerships, foundations)
- **Multi-generational** (considering children's and grandchildren's brackets)
- **Multi-jurisdictional** (state, federal, international)
- **Proactive** (implementing strategies before triggering events)

## 4. Private Investment Access (PE, VC, Direct Deals)

### The Private Markets Advantage

According to Campden Wealth's 2024-2025 research:
- **Private market investments represent 29-30% of average family office portfolios**
- **88% of North American family offices have exposure to private markets**
- Private equity funds are the largest component
- Direct private equity is the most popular asset class for *new* investment

### Types of Private Investment Access

**Private Equity Funds**
- Access to top-quartile PE managers (often closed to new investors)
- Lower minimums through MFO aggregation ($250K-$1M vs $5-10M individually)
- Co-investment opportunities alongside funds
- Secondary market access

**Venture Capital**
- Early-stage access through VC relationships
- Co-investment in individual deals
- Special purpose vehicles (SPVs) for specific opportunities
- Access to oversubscribed funds

**Direct Private Equity**
- Direct investment in operating companies
- Control or significant minority positions
- Proprietary deal flow through MFO networks
- Independent sponsor relationships

**Private Credit**
- Direct lending opportunities
- Mezzanine financing
- Distressed debt
- 39% of family offices planned to *increase* private credit allocation (2024)

**Real Estate**
- Direct property ownership
- Real estate funds and syndicates
- Development projects
- Operating real estate businesses
- 90%+ of family office real estate is in domestic markets

### Access Mechanisms

**Economies of Scale**
MFOs aggregate capital across client families, achieving:
- Lower minimums per family
- Better fee terms (often 50-100 bps below standard)
- Capacity access in oversubscribed funds
- Co-investment rights

**Deal Flow Networks**
- Proprietary deal sourcing through MFO networks
- Family-to-family co-investment
- Independent sponsor relationships
- Direct sourcing capabilities

**Due Diligence Infrastructure**
- In-house investment teams for deal evaluation
- Ongoing manager monitoring
- Portfolio construction across illiquid holdings
- Liquidity management for capital calls

## 5. The $50M+ Family Experience

### What Changes at $50M+

**Service Model Shift**
- Dedicated relationship manager (vs. pooled service)
- Direct access to specialists (vs. generalist advisors)
- Proactive outreach (vs. reactive)
- Custom solutions (vs. off-the-shelf products)

**Investment Access**
- Alternative investment access (PE, VC, hedge funds)
- Institutional share classes with lower fees
- Direct investment opportunities
- Co-investment rights

**Tax Sophistication**
- Multi-year planning horizons
- Entity structuring (trusts, partnerships, LLCs)
- Advanced strategies (GRATs, IDGTs, CRTs)
- Coordination across multiple entities

**Family Complexity Support**
- Multi-generational planning
- Family governance frameworks
- Next-generation education programs
- Succession planning support

### Minimum Thresholds by Service Type

| Service Model | Typical Minimum | Annual Cost Range |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Traditional Wealth Management | $500K - $1M | 0.5-1.5% of AUM |
| Private Bank | $1M - $5M | 0.5-1.0% of AUM |
| Multi-Family Office | $25M - $50M | 0.5-1.0% of AUM + flat fees |
| Single Family Office | $100M - $500M | $1M - $10M+ annually |

### The $50M Experience vs. $5M

| Dimension | $5M Portfolio | $50M Portfolio |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Advisor Ratio** | 1:200+ clients | 1:10-30 families |
| **Service Frequency** | Quarterly reviews | Weekly/monthly contact |
| **Tax Planning** | Basic optimization | Advanced entity strategies |
| **Estate Planning** | Referral to attorney | Integrated planning |
| **Private Investments** | Limited/none | Full alternative access |
| **Family Services** | None | Governance, education, lifestyle |
| **Reporting** | Custodian statements | Consolidated all-asset reporting |

## 6. Democratization Opportunities

### Elements That Can Be Democratized

**Technology-Enabled Services**
1. **Consolidated Reporting**
   - Modern platforms (Addepar, Black Diamond, Orion) can serve smaller accounts
   - Opportunity: Build aggregation tools at $1M+ threshold
   
2. **Tax-Loss Harvesting**
   - Automated through direct indexing (Wealthfront, Betterment, parametric strategies)
   - Now available at $100K+ minimums

3. **Estate Planning Technology**
   - Trust & Will, Fabric, Tomorrow offer basic estate planning
   - Gap: Complex trust structures still require attorneys

4. **Financial Planning**
   - Comprehensive planning at lower thresholds via RIAs
   - Fee-only planners starting at $3,000-$10,000 annually

**Fractional/Pooled Access**
1. **Private Equity/VC**
   - Platforms: iCapital, CAIS, Moonfare lowering minimums to $25K-$50K
   - Interval funds providing quarterly liquidity
   - Regulation A+ offerings for non-accredited investors

2. **Real Estate**
   - Fundrise, RealtyMogul, CrowdStreet at $1K-$50K minimums
   - REIT structures for liquidity

3. **Alternative Investments**
   - Semi-liquid alternatives in '40 Act funds
   - Liquid alternatives replicating hedge fund strategies

**Coordination & Integration**
1. **Virtual Family Office (VFO)**
   - Lean model: 2-4 professionals + outsourced specialists
   - Can serve families at $10M-$50M
   - 40%+ cost savings vs. full-service MFO

2. **RIA Consortiums**
   - Independent RIAs pooling resources for alternative access
   - Shared compliance, technology, and investment infrastructure

### What Remains Exclusive

**Still Requires $50M+ Scale:**
- True bespoke portfolio construction
- Direct co-investment access
- Complex multi-entity tax strategies
- Full-time dedicated team
- Proprietary deal flow
- Family governance specialists
- Multi-jurisdictional tax optimization

**Structural Barriers:**
- SEC accredited investor requirements ($1M+ net worth or $200K+ income)
- Qualified purchaser requirements ($5M+ investments) for many PE funds
- Minimum investment sizes for institutional managers
- Regulatory complexity of private offerings

### Emerging Democratization Trends

1. **Roboadvisors Moving Upmarket**
   - Betterment, Wealthfront adding tax-loss harvesting, planning
   - Vanguard Personal Advisor Services at 0.30%

2. **RIA Aggregators**
   - Focus Financial, Creative Planning, Mariner creating scale
   - Better alternative access, lower costs

3. **Direct Indexing**
   - Personalized tax management at $100K+ levels
   - Parametric, Canvas, Natixis offerings

4. **RegTech & WealthTech**
   - Automated compliance reducing operational costs
   - AI-powered planning and analysis

## 7. Cost Structure of MFO Services

### Operating Cost Benchmarks

**By AUM Level (2024-2025 Data)**

| AUM Range | Avg Annual Cost | Cost as % of AUM |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| $50M - $250M | $400K - $1M | 0.80% - 1.05% |
| $250M - $500M | $1M - $2M | 0.40% - 0.80% |
| $500M - $1B | $2M - $4M | 0.36% - 0.40% |
| $1B+ | $4M - $6M+ | 0.30% - 0.40% |

*Source: J.P. Morgan 2024, Campden Wealth 2024-2025, UBS 2024*

**Median Costs:**
- Average family office operating costs: **$3.2 million annually** (J.P. Morgan 2024)
- Median for $1B+ offices: **$4.2 million annually**
- Smaller offices ($50M-$500M): **$400,000 median**, half spent under $1M

### Cost Components

**Staff Compensation: 50-67% of Operating Budget**
- CEO/Family Office Director: $200K - $500K+
- Chief Investment Officer: $337K (small) to $821K (large)
- Investment professionals: $150K - $400K
- Operations/admin: $75K - $150K
- Low client-to-employee ratio: ~3:1

**Investment Management: ~43% of Total Costs**
- External manager fees: 0.50% - 2.0% (plus carry)
- Custodial fees
- Trading costs
- Research subscriptions
- Due diligence expenses

**Technology: 5-15% of Operating Budget**
- Portfolio management systems: $50K - $200K/year
- Reporting platforms
- Cybersecurity infrastructure
- Client portals

**External Advisors: $100K - $500K+ Annually**
- Legal counsel
- Tax specialists
- Estate planning attorneys
- 80% of family offices use external advisors (90%+ for smaller offices)

### Fee Structures

**Asset-Based Fees**
- Typical range: 0.5% - 2.0% of AUM
- Lower rates for larger asset pools
- Industry average: ~50 basis points for investment management alone

**Fixed/Flat Fees**
- Annual retainer based on scope of services
- Provides cost predictability
- Common range: $50K - $500K annually for defined services

**Hybrid Models**
- Base flat fee + AUM percentage
- Base + performance fee
- Tiered structures declining with AUM growth

**All-In Costs (Investment + Operations)**
- Total cost of wealth management: 1.0% - 2.5% of AUM
- Includes investment fees, operations, external advisors, taxes
- Scale dramatically improves economics

### Cost Efficiency Strategies

1. **Right-Size Staffing**
   - Lean core team (2-4) with outsourced specialists
   - Generalists who coordinate vs. specialists for everything

2. **Technology Leverage**
   - Automated reporting reduces admin headcount
   - AI for research and analysis
   - 69% now use automated investment reporting (up from 46%)

3. **Strategic Outsourcing**
   - Investment management to external managers
   - Tax/legal to specialists on demand
   - ~40% of smaller offices outsource investment management

4. **Virtual Family Office Model**
   - Fully remote, tech-enabled
   - Can reduce costs 40%+ vs. full-service
   - Viable for $10M - $100M families

## Key Takeaways

### For Families Considering MFO Services

1. **Economic viability threshold:** $25M minimum, optimal at $100M+
2. **All-in costs:** Expect 0.75% - 1.50% of AUM annually
3. **Key differentiator:** Integration and coordination, not just investment returns
4. **Alternative access:** Major value driver at 29-30% of portfolios
5. **Succession planning:** 47% expect generational transition within 10 years

### For Democratization Strategy

1. **Technology-enabled services** are most easily democratized
2. **Pooled access to alternatives** expanding rapidly to lower thresholds
3. **Virtual family office** model viable at $10M+ for 40% cost savings
4. **Structural barriers** (accreditation, minimums) remain for private markets
5. **Integration and coordination** remain the hardest to replicate at scale

### Industry Trends (2025)

- 29% now use generative AI (vs. 11% in 2024)
- Private markets remain largest asset class at 29% of portfolios
- Expected returns moderated to 5% (down from 11%)
- 69% have succession plans (up from 53%)
- Improving liquidity (48%) and de-risking (33%) top priorities
