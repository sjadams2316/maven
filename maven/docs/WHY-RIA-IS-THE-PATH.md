## The Wirehouse Model is Obsolete

Let's be honest about what wirehouses actually provided:
- Brand name and trust
- Research and insights
- Product access
- Compliance infrastructure
- Training and support

Now let's be honest about what's changed:
- **Research is commoditized** — Everyone has access to the same information
- **Product access is democratized** — ETFs, direct indexing, alternatives available to any RIA
- **Compliance can be outsourced** — Turnkey solutions at a fraction of wirehouse overhead
- **Technology?** — They're running systems designed in 2005, maybe 2010
- **The brand?** — Millennials and Gen Z don't care that it says "Merrill" on the door

What wirehouses actually provide in 2026:
- A logo
- Bureaucracy
- Conflicts of interest (proprietary product pressure)
- A 55-65% haircut on everything advisors produce

The emperor has no clothes. Top advisors are paying half their income for... what exactly?

**"Nothing happens, then everything happens."**

This is the classic disruption pattern. Clients tolerate mediocre experiences — quarterly PDF statements, clunky portals, phone calls to check balances — because they don't know what's possible.

The moment they see what's possible, the game changes.

The moment a client experiences real-time portfolio intelligence, AI-powered planning, and an interface built for 2026 instead of 2006 — they can't unsee it. The wirehouse experience suddenly feels like using a flip phone.

The wirehouses aren't evolving. They're maintaining. And in technology, maintaining is dying slowly.
